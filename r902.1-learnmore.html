R902.1

Most Restrictive Codes Apply

The specific code for R902.1 states:

"Where there is a conflict between a general requirement and a specific requirement, the specific requirement shall be applicable. Where, in any specific case, different sections of this code specify different materials, methods of construction or other requirements, the most restrictive SHALL govern."

So how can this apply to a roof for a hail or wind claim?

For example, in the document by GAF on the requirements for overlapping felt it specifically says that you need to remove the nails from the top row of shingles so you can overlap the felt by 6". Then it says to re-nail the shingles into place and hand seal them.

In R904.1 it states that you need to follow the manufacture installation instructions AND "the applicable provisions of R905."

The GAF document would be considered the manufacturer installation instructions and should be okay right? However, in R907.5 for the 2012 IRC and R908.5 for the other years it specifically states:

"Aggregate surfacing materials shall not be re-installed."

If we are taking the nails out of the shingles and then re-nailing them back into the roof, this would be considered re-using the shingle and the code above says we cannot re-use the shingle as the shingle would be considered an aggregate surfacing material.

So how is this conflict resolved? By whichever is stricter as outlined in R902.1. Clearly, the IRC provision to not re-use the shingle is stricter than the manufacturer installation instructions to re-use them. So in this case we would NOT re-use the shingle.
